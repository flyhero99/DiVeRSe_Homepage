Question

Ali is a dean of a private school where he teaches one class. John is also a dean of a public school. John has two classes in his school. Each class has 1/8 the capacity of Ali’s class which has the capacity of 120 students. What is the combined capacity of both schools?

Solution

Method: Self-Consistency

Question: Siobhan has 2 fewer jewels than Aaron. Aaron has 5 more jewels than half of Raymond's jewels. If Raymond has 40 jewels, how many jewels does Siobhan have? Self: Aaron has 2 more jewels than half of Raymond's jewels. So Aaron has 40 / 2 + 2 = <<40/2+2=22 >> 22 jewels%% Siobhan has 2 fewer jewels than Aaron so she has 22 - 2 = <<22-2=20 >> 20 jewels%% #### 20 Cross: Raymond has 40 jewels.%% Half of Raymond's jewels is 40/2=<<40/2=20 >> 20%% Aaron has 5 more jewels than half of Raymond's jewels, so he has 20+5=<<20+5=25 >> 25 jewels.%% Siobhan has 2 fewer jewels than Aaron, so she has 25-2=<<25-2=23 >> 23 jewels.%% #### 23

The combined capacity of the two schools is 120 students + 30 students = 150 students.

Answer: 150 ❌

Question

Ali is a dean of a private school where he teaches one class. John is also a dean of a public school. John has two classes in his school. Each class has 1/8 the capacity of Ali’s class which has the capacity of 120 students. What is the combined capacity of both schools?

Solution

Method: Self-Consistency

Q: Self: The total number of games they played is 22, and they won 8 more than they lost, so the number of games they won is 22 - 8 = <<22-8=14 >> 14 games.%% #### 14 Cross:

Let w be the number of games they won.

Then they lost w-8 games.

So they played w + (w-8) games.

w + (w-8) = 22.

2w - 8 = 22.

2w = 30.

w = 15.

So they won 15 games.

Answer: 15 ✅
