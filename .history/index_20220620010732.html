Question

Siobhan has 2 fewer jewels than Aaron. Aaron has 5 more jewels than half of Raymond's jewels. If Raymond has 40 jewels, how many jewels does Siobhan have?

Solution

Method: Self-Consistency

Aaron has 2 more jewels than half of Raymond's jewels.

So Aaron has 40 / 2 + 2 = 22 jewels.

Siobhan has 2 fewer jewels than Aaron so she has 22 - 2 = 20 jewels.

Answer: 20 ❌

Method: DiVeRSe

Raymond has 40 jewels.

Half of Raymond's jewels is 40 / 2 = 20.

Aaron has 5 more jewels than half of Raymond's jewels, so he has 20 + 5 = 25 jewels.

Siobhan has 2 fewer jewels than Aaron, so she has 25 - 2 = 23 jewels.

Answer: 23 ✅

Question

A football team played 22 games. They won 8 more than they lost. How many did they win?

Solution

Method: Self-Consistency

The total number of games they played is 22, and they won 8 more than they lost, so the number of games they won is 22 - 8 = 14 games.

Answer: 14 ❌

Method: DiVeRSe

Let w be the number of games they won.

Then they lost w-8 games.

So they played w + (w-8) games.

w + (w-8) = 22.

2w - 8 = 22.

2w = 30.

w = 15.

So they won 15 games.

Answer: 15 ✅

Question

There are twice as many boys as girls at Dr. Wertz's school. If there are 60 girls and 5 students to every teacher, how many teachers are there?

Solution

Method: Self-Consistency

There are 120 boys at Dr. Wertz's school.

There are 60 + 120 = 180 students total.

There are 180 / 5 = 36 students per teacher.

Thus, there are 180 / 36 = 5 teachers.

Answer: 5 ❌

Method: DiVeRSe

The number of boys is 2 * 60 = 120.

There are 60 + 120 = 180 students total.

Each teacher has 5 students, so 180 / 5 = 36 teachers.

Answer: 36 ✅
